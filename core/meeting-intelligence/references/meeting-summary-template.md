# Meeting Summary Template

## Summary Format

```
MEETING SUMMARY
===============
Meeting:   <title>
Date:      YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM UTC
Type:      <meeting type>
Duration:  N minutes
Prepared by: meeting-intelligence skill
Status:    DRAFT — requires [attendee] review before distribution

ATTENDANCE
  Present:  [name (role), name (role), ...]
  Absent:   [name (role), ...]

HEADLINE
  <One sentence describing the most important outcome of this meeting>

DECISIONS
  1. [Decision stated clearly and completely]
     Owner: [decision maker]
  2. [Decision stated clearly and completely]
  ...

ACTION ITEMS
  | # | Task | Owner | Due Date | Priority |
  |---|------|-------|----------|----------|
  | 1 | [task] | [name] | YYYY-MM-DD | High |
  | 2 | [task] | [name] | YYYY-MM-DD | Medium |
  ...

OPEN QUESTIONS
  1. [Question] → Owner: [name] → Due: YYYY-MM-DD
  2. [Question] → Deferred to next meeting
  ...

KEY DISCUSSION POINTS
  (Optional — include only for complex/important meetings)
  - [Key topic discussed and outcome]
  - [Key topic discussed and outcome]

NEXT MEETING
  Date: YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM UTC
  Agenda items: [list if known]
```

## Distribution Rules

| Meeting Type | Distribution | Approval Before Send |
|---|---|---|
| Standup | Team Slack channel | None (action items only) |
| Sprint planning | Team + stakeholders | None |
| Architecture review | Engineering + product | Tech lead review |
| Board / investor | Board members | CEO + CFO approval |
| Customer meeting | Internal participants + customer contacts | Account owner approval |
| 1:1 | Private (shared with manager only) | Both participants |
| All-hands | All-company | CEO/Comms review |

---

## Draft vs Final

**Draft** (auto-generated by meeting-intelligence):
- Produced within 30 minutes of meeting end
- Marked clearly as DRAFT
- Sent to meeting organizer for review

**Final**:
- Reviewed and approved by the designated reviewer
- Sent to distribution list
- Saved to knowledge-graph and meeting record

---

## Quality Checks Before Distribution

- [ ] All decisions are stated as complete sentences (not fragments)
- [ ] All action items have: task, owner (full name), due date
- [ ] Open questions have an assigned owner
- [ ] No PII or sensitive content (salary, performance) in non-private distributions
- [ ] For customer meetings: no internal pricing/margin information included
- [ ] For board meetings: CFO/CEO reviewed and approved

---

## Headline Writing Guide

The headline is the single most important sentence — it should answer:
"What happened in this meeting that matters?"

**Good headlines**:
- "Board approved Series B term sheet; closing targeted for June 15"
- "Architecture decision: move to event-driven messaging via Kafka for all cross-service communication"
- "Sprint 23 committed: 8 user stories including the new billing flow redesign"

**Poor headlines**:
- "We had a productive meeting"
- "Many topics were discussed"
- "Sprint planning was completed"
